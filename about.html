Introduction

The Government of Bangladesh has launched an effort to develop a new Economic Zone (EZ) paradigm for Bangladesh. The effort aims to maximize the potential direct and indirect impact of EZ’s through a more modern, generalized regime, and draws from numerous successful examples from around the world as well as Bangladesh's own experience with the EPZ model. By improving the structure and performance of EZ’s, the government hopes that these entities can generate new foreign and local investment flows, which will spillover to local firms aiming to have a ripple effect through local economies, in the form of improving access to new technologies, strengthening linkages between the private sector and education system, improving firm performance and growth, and in general creating new economic opportunities.

One of the Government’s key legislative accomplishments was to create two authorities to spearhead the push towards industrialization with greater involvement of the private sector. The Bangladesh Economic Zones Authority Act and the Bangladesh Hi-Tech Park Authority Act—both created in 2010—established two new semi-autonomous agencies—the Bangladesh Economic Zones Authority (BEZA) and the Bangladesh Hi-Tech Park Authority (BHTPA)—tasked to oversee the expansion of economic zones (EZs) and hi-tech parks (HTPs) in the country.

Economic context

Current estimates of the size of Bangladesh’s digital economy range from 5 percent to 15 percent of GDP. Information, communication, and technology (ICT)-related exports were estimated at US$1 billion in 2019. Nearly 300,000 Bangladeshi professionals are able to code software, design software applications and serve clients at home and abroad through peer-to-peer digital marketplaces, compared to less than 10,000 five years ago. The digital economy is increasingly affecting traditional industries. For example, more traditional businesses are adapting to digital platforms and use ITS and ITES to improve operations. There is also an increasing demand for facilities offering connectivity, funding, market access, and physical and virtual workspaces for digital entrepreneurs. In fact, estimates suggest that four out of five ITS and ITES businesses operate from non-commercial or residential areas. Additionally, the sector has the potential to be an important job creator for educated women. The share of female employees varies across STPs with an average of 33 percent. STPs provide a unique opportunity to promote female employment and entrepreneurship in the ITS and ITES sector, which is generally lower than in some other sectors.

Project overview

The BHTPA is planning to establish incubation centers within the STPs and select technical universities to strengthen the entrepreneurship ecosystem and encourage digital entrepreneurship in Bangladesh. Bangladesh needs to strengthen the pipeline of ITS and ITES companies that have the size and capabilities to leverage private equity and venture capital for growth. A thriving ITS-ITES sector would not only generate export revenue and high paying jobs but also raise productivity in the domestic economy. Utilizing private sector expertise and integrating incubation, acceleration and mentorship programs based on good international practices could support in achieving these outcomes.

PRIDE Project Development Objective

The PDO is to promote private investment, job creation, and environmental sustainability in participating economic zones and software technology parks in Bangladesh.

Project Components

The PRIDE project has four components, and the BHTPA will be responsible for implementing Component 4: Strengthening the Digital Entrepreneurship and Innovation Ecosystem.

Component 4

which will be implemented by the BHTPA, aims to strengthen the foundation of the digital entrepreneurship and innovation ecosystem in Bangladesh and take advantage of the increasing global and domestic demand for digital services further ramped up by the Covid-19 pandemic. It will create the country’s largest agglomeration of ITS and ITES SMEs in Dhaka’s Vision 2021 Tower Software Technology Park (STP), with the larger goals of creating entrepreneurship hubs, increasing market entry and growth rates of digital startups and SMEs, as well as promoting digital entrepreneurship among young professionals and women. The Component is comprised of three programs. First, it will establish an accelerator program focusing on both startups and scale-ups, to be run out of the STPs. Second, it will pilot entrepreneurship and innovation hubs in Bangladesh’s leading technological universities and business schools. Doing so will also offer accredited and rapid training programs to budding entrepreneurs and managers in the tech field. Third, it will implement a media program to help change attitudes surrounding entrepreneurship in Bangladesh, which will aim to ultimately attract more youth, women, and professionals to consider become entrepreneurs.

The objective of the assignment

Design, establish and operate programs for entrepreneurs in Bangladesh’s digital economy. The services offered will include incubation and acceleration support, market entry and market expansion advisory, and investment readiness support to help digital entrepreneurs access external sources of finance. The services will be divided into programs that target early-stage entrepreneurs (startup program) as well as established digital businesses (scale-up program). The startup program will target to incubate 150-200 early-stage firms (startups) per year, while the Scale-up programs will target 40-60 established and growth oriented digital businesses (scale-ups) annually and help them to plan and manage their growth strategy and increase their level of investment readiness over a six-month support period.

Services will be offered to entrepreneurs in Software Technology Parks (STPs) through a hub-and-spoke model. The initial main offices for operations (i.e., the hub) will be in Dhaka and Chattogram, followed by a gradual roll-out elsewhere (Jessore, Kaliakoir, Rajshahi, and other STPs with limited local staffing complemented by virtual programming). Entrepreneurs located outside the STPs will be able to access programs to create linkages and network effects in addition to a prospective pipeline for new tenants.
